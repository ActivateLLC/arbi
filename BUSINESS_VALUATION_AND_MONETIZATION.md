## üí∞ BUSINESS VALUATION

### SaaS Valuation Multiples

**Industry Standard:**
- Early Stage (Pre-revenue): 2-4x ARR
- Growth Stage (Profitable): 5-8x ARR
- Mature/High Growth: 8-15x ARR
- Market Leader: 15-25x ARR

**Key Metrics That Affect Valuation:**
- **ARR Growth Rate:** >100% YoY = higher multiple
- **Churn Rate:** <5% monthly = higher multiple
- **Gross Margin:** >80% = higher multiple
- **CAC Payback:** <12 months = higher multiple
- **LTV/CAC Ratio:** >3:1 = higher multiple
- **Net Revenue Retention:** >100% = higher multiple

## üî• MODEL 3: Revenue Share

**Structure:** Take 5-15% of user profits

**Example:**
- User makes $10,000/month profit from arbitrage
- You take 10% = $1,000/month from that user
- If you have 100 profitable users averaging $5,000/month
- Your revenue: 100 √ó $5,000 √ó 10% = $50,000/month

**Revenue Projections:**
```
Year 1:
- 100 users avg $3,000/month profit √ó 10% = $30,000/month MRR
- ARR: $360,000

Year 2:
- 300 users avg $5,000/month profit √ó 10% = $150,000/month MRR
- ARR: $1,800,000

Year 3:
- 800 users avg $7,000/month profit √ó 10% = $560,000/month MRR
- ARR: $6,720,000
```

**Valuation:**
- Similar to SaaS (recurring revenue)
- 5-10x ARR
- Year 1: $1,800,000 - $3,600,000
- Year 2: $9,000,000 - $18,000,000
- Year 3: $33,600,000 - $67,200,000

**Pros:**
- Aligned incentives (you only earn when they earn)
- Can charge higher % because it's risk-free for them
- Scales with user success

**Cons:**
- Requires profit tracking/verification
- Users may resist sharing profit data
- Complex accounting
- Potential for fraud
